Timeline of Elder Scrolls official lore

Before the realm of Mundus existed, the overlap between the primal forces Anu (stasis, known as Ahnurr to the Khajiit) and Padomay (change, known as Fadomai) formed the Aurbis, or existence. Interplay between Anu and Padomay and the Aurbis respectively generated Anui-El (known as Satak to the Redguard)–the soul of all living things and everlasting light–and Sithis (known as Akal to the Redguard)–emptiness and void. From Anui-El came Auri-El–the First Deity, who is the Dragon God of Time (known as Akatosh to most of the human races, Alkosh to the Khajiit, and Ruptga to the Redguards), and from Sithis came Lorkhan. Numerous et'ada (or spirits) sprang into being independently out of the Aurbis.

Lorkhan persuaded some of the et'ada to give part of themselves to create the material plane of Mundus, with the one called Magnus being its architect. These became known as Aedra and formed eight planets and the world Nirn with its physical laws. Daedra, et'ada who did not help creating Mundus, exist in realms collectively known as Oblivion that separate Mundus from Aetherius, the Immortal Plane where the et'ada originated, to where the souls of the dead are purported to go, and the origin of all magic.

Because they had been tricked into giving up much of their power, the Aedra met and decided to punish Lorkhan. The et'ada Trinimac, a great warrior, tore Lorkhan's heart out and gave it to Auri-El, who fired it into the sea with a bow. Its impact created the volcano known as Red Mountain on Vvardenfell (which imbued Nirn with Lorkhan's spirit and selfishness), while Lorkhan's body remained floating in the sky as the moons Masser and Secunda (Jone and Jode to the Bosmer).

Magnus, disgusted by Mundus' corrupted nature, left for Aetherius and ripped a hole in Oblivion, which is seen as the sun. Other et'ada known as Magna Ge followed Magnus, leaving smaller holes which are the stars.
